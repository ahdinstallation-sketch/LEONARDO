AHD Group · Whole-company simulator

Fix follow-up first — the path from −283k to +1.6M/mo

Where we stand: on a cash basis the whole group runs ≈ −283k/mo (ties to the dashboard’s −284k) — operations roughly break even, but debt service drains the rest. The single biggest lever isn’t more ad spend, it’s converting the leads you already pay for: lifting lead→measurement follow-up from 4.67% to 7% alone turns −283k into +465k. Marketing helps too, but with diminishing returns — there’s a sweet spot (≈954k/mo), not a money-printer. Recommended package (the green ★ markers): spend at the sweet spot, follow-up to 7%, Designy material 49→40%, hold discounts → ≈ +1.6M/mo. Hit ★ Set all to recommended to load it.

Engine: the measured AHD lead-to-contract pipeline (1,072 EGP/lead, 4.67% lead→measurement, 50% measurement→contract). Revenue baseline = actual 2026 cash-in (≈5.43M AHD/mo), most of it organic; ad spend and follow-up scale the incremental slice. Overhead = group average-expenses budget (~3.9M/mo) on a 2025 order-basis split; resale streams (counters, appliances, furniture/chairs) ride on AHD sales. Two honest caveats: AHD discount barely moves profit here (volume doesn’t follow price, E≈0), and installments are left at ×1 because the model counts cheques as net-new volume (optimistic). A decision aid, not management accounts.
