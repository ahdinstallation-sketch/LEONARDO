Leonardo Kitchen — two dials: discount & material

Everything else is fixed at Leonardo’s 2025 reality. You move only discount and material %. Volume isn’t a dial — it follows the discount automatically (Designy’s demand elasticity, E≈4.9): discount more and you sell more, but every module earns less. Watch where the monthly profit lands.

Fixed inputs (Leonardo 2025 actuals): list price 11,200/module · overhead pool 0.26M/month (≈17% of Designy’s 1.49M line overhead, by revenue share) · base volume 25/month at the 32.4% base discount. A “unit” = one cabinet/module.

Monthly profit as you slide the discount — gold = your material %, green dashed = the 40% target. The bold line is break-even (zero). The curve climbs as discount buys volume, but watch where it tops out.

Volume = 25 × (1 + 4.9 × (discount − 32.4%)), capped 5–65/mo (Designy elasticity & capacity band). Net price = 11,200 × (1 − discount). Gross profit/module = net × (1 − material%). Overhead/module = 260,000 ÷ volume. Net profit/module = gross profit/module − overhead/module. Total/month = gross profit/module × volume − 260,000. Material % is of net sales (Badr costing basis).
